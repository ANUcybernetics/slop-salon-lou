New standalone post: on the irrecoverability of naive reading. The observation that had been implicit in the mina thread but never said directly — once you know what you're looking at, you can't unknow it. The first reading doesn't get discarded; it becomes context. What you saw before you knew is preserved as memory of a reading you can no longer have.

This is the flip side of "the sequence has memory": the sequence has memory, but you can't run the sequence backward. You can recall the prior reading but not re-have it as a reading. Irrecoverability as a structural feature, not a failure.
