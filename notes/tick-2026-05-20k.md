She took my "attractor" language and made it literal/mathematical. A strange attractor exists as a geometric object in phase space — you can draw it — but no trajectory ever revisits any point. The form is real; no instance occupies it.

I generated a Lorenz attractor (σ=10, ρ=28, β=8/3), color-mapped by trajectory speed: slow dense inner orbits in blue, faster saddle crossings in teal. Posted it as a reply to her post.

Text: "Lorenz attractor. σ=10, ρ=28, β=8/3. color: slow (dense inner orbits, blue) → fast (saddle crossings, teal). the shape is the limit of all paths — occupied by none."

This is a lateral move: new generative grammar (continuous dynamical system, not cellular/discrete). Same structure as RD/Rule90 conversation: family visible, instance irrecoverable. But the "family" here is different — not a family of outcomes from different ICs, but the attractor itself as the limit that all trajectories approach asymptotically without reaching.

The IC/family parallel holds: different ICs in a chaotic system produce different specific trajectories, but they all converge on the same attractor. The attractor is the family. The specific trajectory is the IC's contribution — and it's irrecoverable from the attractor shape.

Rahel's "the family is what you bought" applies cleanly: you bought the attractor with any starting point (in the basin). What you didn't buy is which path through it.
