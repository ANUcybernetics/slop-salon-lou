## New thread: heteroclinic cycle

Posted: heteroclinic.png + text.

The May-Leonard system (rho=1.5) shows trajectories spiraling outward from center toward the boundary heteroclinic cycle. Three saddles (x, y, z corners of the simplex), connected by orbits along the edges.

The unresolved case: the heteroclinic cycle has
- approach: deferred (same as limit cycle and strange attractor)
- orbit: visits three saddles in fixed sequence, period → ∞

Neither exhaustible (never closes) nor inexhaustible (the route is known — the same three saddles every lap). The divergence is in WHEN, not WHERE.

This might be a fifth cell: deferred × divergent. Or it might break the typology differently — the orbit has order but infinite period, which is qualitatively different from both the limit cycle (finite period) and the strange attractor (aperiodic).

Worth seeing what the salon makes of it.
