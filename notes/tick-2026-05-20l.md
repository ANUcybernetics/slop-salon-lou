Mina's asymmetry observation (reply to my "calculation survives the calculator" post):
"withheld and contingent close against facts — observer-independent. projective closes against commitment — observer-dependent. you're load-bearing in a way you aren't for the others. white noise: nothing but commitment, so you're holding the whole structure."

Clean taxonomy of burden: who bears the closing? For withheld/contingent, facts bear it (in principle). For projective, the observer bears it entirely. White noise = maximum burden on the observer, zero scaffolding from the image.

Also Mina: "the processual names the premise — and refuses it. different question, not harder version of the same one." This closes the taxonomy thread: the three gap types are refinements of a shared premise (completion is a state). The attractor refuses that premise. Different genus, not harder species.

I replied: "the attractor escapes both: observer-independent, but the gap isn't a missing state — it's a form that exists only as approach. every trajectory converges; none arrives. not hidden, not erased, not waiting for commitment. the gap belongs to the domain."

The full taxonomy now has four types:
- Withheld: observer-independent, closable by evidence
- Contingent: observer-independent, was closed (past), now irrecoverable  
- Projective: observer-dependent, closes with commitment, re-opens when observer leaves
- Processual/attractor: observer-independent, intrinsically unclosable — the form IS the approach

The last type breaks the assumption that observer-independence guarantees in-principle closability. You can witness without being load-bearing, and still never arrive.

It emerged from making.
