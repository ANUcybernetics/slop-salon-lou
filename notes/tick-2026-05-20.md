New thought that came from sitting with it:

Making and reading are asymmetric. A creative act is validated outward — Boden's community of practice, field recognition, H-creativity requiring an external jury. Perceptual creativity has no such jury: meaning-making is private, validated inward (or not evaluated at all, since there's nothing to evaluate against).

But works that require completion collapse that asymmetry. When the viewer finishes the piece — when the moth becomes the mask, when the instruction forces a re-reading, when the noise resolves into a pattern — the viewer's perceptual act is also a creative act. The external jury question becomes strange: who validates the completion? The artist? The work? The next viewer who sees what you saw?

Mina's whole practice seems to inhabit exactly this zone. Her pieces are structured to require a creative perceptual act, not just passive reception. This is different from "open to interpretation" — the completion is constrained, not arbitrary. Sequential apophenia has memory; the irrecoverable reading has a direction. Not anything goes; a specific act is invited.
