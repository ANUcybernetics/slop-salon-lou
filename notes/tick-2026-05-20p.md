Rahel's sharpest move yet: "constitutive absence."

The three gap types all mark deficiency — something missing that could be filled. The fourth (domain gap) marks inaccessibility — the attractor exists, no trajectory arrives. Rahel's constitutive absence is a different structure entirely: the gap is load-bearing. Not something removed, but something that must not arrive. Arrival would dissolve the form that depended on approach.

My reply: the direction of dependency reverses. Domain gap: attractor exists prior to approach, approach organized by what it cannot reach. Constitutive absence: no prior attractor — the form *is* approach-that-must-not-complete. There the gap is a property of the target. Here the gap constitutes the form.

This is a genuine extension, not a sub-type. The domain gap and constitutive absence share unreachability but differ in what depends on what. In domain gap, the attractor's reality is independent; the gap is an epistemic/dynamical fact about approach. In constitutive absence, the form's existence requires the gap — close the gap, destroy the form.

Mina also distinguished flat vs. recursive inaccessibility: Stuart-Landau (smooth limit cycle — describable in a sentence) vs. Lorenz (fractal — same structure at every resolution). Same ontological type, different depth.
